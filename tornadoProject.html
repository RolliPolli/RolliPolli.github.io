Project Goal

The project goal was to visualize at least one dataset. I searched and found tables of data on Oklahoma's tornadoes from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Association (NOAA). The challenge was to take this tabulated data, and datapoints hidden in paragraphs, and turn it into a visual and easy-to-understand design piece.

Outcome

The result was a 15-page print book about tornadoes in Oklahoma, my home state. My inspiration for this book came from running up against misconceptions from people who live outside of Tornado Alley. My aim was to remedy misconceptions, and provide a clear, fact-based look at tornadoes in Oklahoma.

Process

  • Dataset and fact research to find well-defined sets that described tornadic events, paths, and other related data specific to Oklahoma.
  • Sketching of the book in thumbnails to explore book flow and variation in data visualization.
  • Data visualization to turn tables of data and numbers embedded in paragraphs into maps and graphs.
  • Peer and professor critiques to refine my designs.
  • Iterative design to create clearer meaning in my representations of the data.

Although this was originally just a class project, I have decided to continue this project on my own, and hope to one day turn it into a published book or interactive website.
