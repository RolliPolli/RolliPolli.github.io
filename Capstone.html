Project Goal

The over-arching goal is to deliver a high-fidelity, user-centered prototype to address major usability issues in Eaton's low-voltage and medium-voltage switchgear line. Switchgear are large metalclad devices that distribute power in places such as hospitals and universities.

The project encompasses 2 semesters. The first semester is 4 months of user research, data synthesis, insights generation, and visioning. The second semester is 2.5 months of iterative design involving prototyping, user testing, refinement.

The deliverables are distinct for the 2 semesters. In the first semester we delivered a 110-page report book of our process, findings, and visions, a 45-minute presentation and Q&A session with our Eaton clients, and facilitated a creative visioning session with our clients. In the second semester, our deliverables will be a high-fidelity prototype of our design, a second report book of our design process and final design, and a final presentation to our Eaton clients.


My Role

My role is to sure that all our designs are informed by our user data and work side-by-side with my teammates to perform research, conduct user testing, iterate on our ideas, and make sure our designs provide good experiences.

Throughout this project, I have been a visual designer for the team through designing our 110-page team research report book for our client, developing our team logo, and designing templates for our documentation and presentations. Also, I have performed user research, data synthesis, and visioning sessions with my team to create innovative solutions that center on the men and women who work with switchgear everyday.

Most recently, I have been developing scenarios, wireframes, test scripts, and performing iterative rapid prototyping and user testing to hone our solution. I am also taking the lead on the design of our summer report book.


Research Process

  • Documentation throughout the process to record all that we've done and learned.
  • Literature review to get familiar with existing domain and analogous research.
  • Interviews and observations with 35 key stakeholders representing all levels of switchgear interaction.
  • Business origami with a power operations manager to visualize an emergency situation.
  • Visioning with Eaton Field Service Engineers to unearth needs and wants on the job.
  • Affinity diagramming of our 600+ datapoints to create meaning and structure of our data.
  • Journey mapping to break down the experience in various typical switchgear events.
  • Cultural mapping to map the influencers for the major stakeholders.
  • Insight generation to unearth the areas that need to be addressed by our designs
  • Visioning with Eaton clients to help with our direction and client ownership.

Design Process

  • Ideation of potential solutions while revisiting our findings and insights
  • Scenario generation and speed dating to gauge usefulness of multiple ideas.
  • Wireframing to draw out and test multiple iterations of our idea.
  • Paper Prototyping with testing to quickly iterate and refine our ideas.
  • Med-fi Prototypes to bring our ideas into a real and believable form, then test them.
  • Speed dating and interviewing to reveal the pros and cons of multiple iterations.

Since this project will continue through August 2014, the above list will continue to grow till the project's end.
