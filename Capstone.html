Project Description

My team is working with Eaton to envision the future of human-switchgear interactions of their low voltage and medium voltage switchgear products, specifically in the commercial sector. A switchgear is a large surge protection and power distribution device made for larger buildings, such as universities and hospitals.


Process

My team and I wanted to learn more about working with switchgears from the perspectives all who touch them throughout the switchgear life cycle. We conducted background research through creating a competitive and literature analysis, talking to 35 subject-matter experts at universities and hospitals, and visiting Eaton's training facility to physically interact with the switchgears ourselves.

To talk to service engineers, electrical consultants, building operations managers, and building electricians we used a variety of techniques including guided storytelling, touchstone tours, business origami, observation with retrospective interview, and visioning sessions. We learned the nature of their daily worklives and their needs and fears while working with switchgears. We then used all this information in our data synthesis so we could understand and address the painpoints in their daily lives.

Talking with the Eaton's UX team to learn about their newest safety dashboard

We synthesized over 600+ data points through affinity diagrams and flow diagrams. Additionally, we drew cultural models to understand the influences for various stakeholders, and we made journey maps to understand in detail the interactions that occur in commissioning and troubleshooting processes. As a team we read aloud and discussed all our findings, so that nothing was overlooked, and that we all understood what we heard and saw.

Setting research goals with the team on the whiteboard

Role Description

I am in charge of making sure that all our designs are informed by our data. So far, I have been a visual designer for the team through designing our 110-page team report book for our client, developing our team logo, designing templates for all of our documentation and presentations. Also, I have been performing user research alongside my teammates, and collaboratively working together to synthesize our data. Most recently I have been visioning with my team to create innovative solutions that center on the men and women who work with switchgears everyday.

In the summer, I will be developing wireframes, paper prototypes, and performing iterative rapid prototyping and user testing to hone our solution. I will also be involved with the design of our summer report book.
