MHCI Capstone Project with Eaton Corporation

Project Description

My four-person team is paired up with Eaton, a power management company who serves various global customers, for our 8-month capstone project. We'll be working with Eaton to improve usability of their low voltage and medium voltage switchgear products. So, what is a switchgear? Well, much like a fusebox in a house, a switchgear is a large surge protection and power distribution device made for buildings such as universities and hospitals. Eaton wants us to look into the future of how service engineers will interact with switchgear in the next 5-10 years. Our aim is to create the future of the switchgear-human interface to enhace the safety, reliability, and the efficiency of power management systems.


Process

At the start of this project, my team and I wanted to learn more about the domain, and learn the current issues within switchgear-human interactions. To get familiar, we conducted background research through creating an exhaustive competitive analysis, talking to several subject-matter experts, and visiting Eaton's training facility to physically interact with the switchgears ourselves.


Talking with the Eaton's UX team

We are currently developing our research plan, which outlines who we are going to talk to and the research methods we are going to employ. These methods include contextual inquiry, interviews, card sorting, business origami, and more to gain a deep understanding of the user's needs so we can more easily design around them.


Setting research goals with the team

Moving forward we will research analogous domains, synthesize our collected data, then create holistic models that fully describe our research findings. We will then create several research-supported visions and discuss with Eaton which solution best fits their wants and needs. Starting in May we will iteratively prototype and test the one vision that Eaton finds the most interesting.


Role Description

I am in charge of making sure that all our designs are informed by our data. So far, I have been a visual designer for the team through developing our team logo, designing templates for all of our documentation and presentations, and developing the design language and overall design for our project books. Also, I have been performing user research alongside my teammates, and am looking forward to taking part in the data synthesis and vision ideation at the end of the semester. In the summer, I will be developing wireframes, paper prototypes, and performing iterative rapid prototyping and testing to hone our solution.
