Bloom iPad Magazine

Project Description

For the final project in my interaction design fundamentals class, I developed an iPad magazine prototype. It is called Bloom, and it is a gardening magazine for kids with a good dose of botany. Its aim is to help children learn about plants and how to care for them. I was inspired by old scientific sketches of plants, but I wanted the content and feel to be approachable for children and their parents. The issue that I prototyped was all about cacti and succulents. The design takes advantage of the unique swipe, touch, and tap features of iPad technology to make the whole experience more interactive, and hopefully more fun.



Techniques

  • Competitive Analysis
  • MoodBoard
  • Design Language
  • Personas
  • Sketching
  • Iterative Design

Duration

  • Oct 2013 - Dec 2013

Process

To kick off this project, I did a competitive analysis* with my team. I wanted to see what the landscape was for gardening magazines. I found that magazines for children in this area was totally unexploited.



Next, I developed a moodboard that would form the mood I wanted for the magazine, and later I translated this into a design language. I also developed two personas that represented the spectrum of users.



Moving on into the development of the magazine, I did several iterations of sketches for the layout and flow of interaction. Lastly, I developed the magazine in InDesign, and through a series of iterations and critiques arrived at my final design. To complete the project, I presented the magazine and its process to my faculty and peers at the end of the semester.
