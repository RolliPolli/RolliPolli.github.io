Bloom iPad Magazine

Project Description

Bloom is a gardening magazine for kids with a good dose of botany. Its aim is to help children learn about plants and how to care for them. I was inspired by old scientific sketches of plants, but I wanted the content and feel to be approachable for children and their parents. The issue that I prototyped was all about cacti and succulents. The design takes advantage of the unique swipe and tap features of iPad technology to make the whole experience more interactive, and hopefully more fun.

Process

To kick-off this project, I did a competitive analysis to see what the landscape was for gardening magazines. I found that children as an audience were a totally unlooked area for gardening magazines. My goal was to make the magazine I would create accessible for children, so I researched children's storybooks, characteristics of typefaces for children, old scientific drawings of plants, succulents and cacti, and other children's magazines.

During this time, I also developed a moodboard to create the look and feel for the magazine from clippings from my research, which I later translated into a design language. To create an embodiment of my target audience, I crafted two personas that represented the spectrum of users: a busy parent and a young child.

Moving on into the development of the magazine, I did several sketches for the layout and flow of interaction. I created design elements and navigation cues which leveraged the iPad swipe and tap gestures to increase engagement for children. After iterating on the layout and design several times, I started developing the magazine in InDesign. Through a series of class critiques and iterations, I arrived at my final design. As a deliverable, I presented the magazine and its process to my faculty and peers at the end of the semester.
