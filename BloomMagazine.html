Bloom iPad Magazine

UI/Interaction Design

Bloom is an interactive gardening magazine for kids with a good dose of botany. Its aim is to teach children and their parents about plants and how to care for them.

Techniques

  • Competitive Analysis
  • MoodBoard
  • Design Language
  • Personas
  • Sketching
  • Wireframing
  • Iterative Design

Technology

  • Adobe InDesign
  • Adobe Illustrator

Duration

  • Oct 2013 - Dec 2013


View PDF of Bloom.


Project Goal

I wanted to design a concept iPad magazine for a young children and their parents who were curious about gardening and plant life. Part of my challenge was to keep my design focused on the audience while taking advantage of the interactive capabilities of the iPad.

Outcome

I was inspired by old scientific illustrations of plants, but I wanted the content and feel to be approachable for children and their parents. I designed it so that parents and children could read the magazine together and both learn something new. The issue of the magazine I designed was all about cacti and succulents. I aimed to use the unique swipe and tap features of iPad technology to make the whole experience more interactive than traditional magazines.


Process Work



I wanted a fun and child-friendly feel, so I made a moodboard from my research clippings.



I crafted a design language from the fun and child-friendly elements in my moodboard.



I designed personas to guide and focus my designs.


I drew out over several iterations the magazine's flow and layout.


Process

  • Competitive analysis to discover the landscape of gardening magazines.
  • Research into storybooks, characteristics of typefaces for children, scientific plants drawings, succulents and cacti, and other children's magazines.
  • Moodboard made from research clippings to create the overall feeling of the magazine.
  • Design language from research and moodboard to inspire the magazine's feel.
  • Persona design for the spectrum of users: a child and a busy mom.
  • Layout and flow sketches for the magazine.
  • Navigational cue design.
  • Iterative design in InDesign through peer and professor critiques.
  • Final peer presentation to talk about my process and my work.
