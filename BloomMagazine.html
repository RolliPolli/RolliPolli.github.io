Bloom iPad Magazine

UI/Interaction Design

Bloom is an interactive gardening magazine for kids with a good dose of botany. Its aim is to teach children about plants and how to care for them.

Project Goal

For this project the goals was to design a 6-page iPad magazine spread and cover for a specific subject and audience, making sure to design for the audience while taking advantage of the interactive capabilities of an iPad.

For this project, I was inspired by old scientific illustrations of plants, but I wanted the content and feel to be approachable for children and their parents. I designed it so that parents and children could read the magazine together and both learn something new. The issue of the magazine I designed was all about cacti and succulents. I aimed to use the unique swipe and tap features of iPad technology to make the whole experience more interactive and engaging.


Process

  • Competitive analysis to discover the landscape of gardening magazines.
  • Research into storybooks, characteristics of typefaces for children, scientific plants drawings, succulents and cacti, and other children's magazines.
  • Moodboard made from research clippings to create the overall feeling of the magazine.
  • Design language from research and moodboard to inspire the magazine's feel.
  • Persona design for the spectrum of users: a child and a busy mom.
  • Layout and flow sketches for the magazine.
  • Navigational cue design.
  • Iterative design in InDesign through peer and professor critiques.
  • Final peer presentation to talk about my process and my work.
